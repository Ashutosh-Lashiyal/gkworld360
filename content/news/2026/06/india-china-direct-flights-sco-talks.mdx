India and China took two notable steps towards normalising relations in April 2026 — the resumption of direct air services between their capitals, and their first bilateral consultations on matters relating to the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO).

## Air China Restarts Non-stop Delhi–Beijing Service

Air China resumed its direct flight operations between New Delhi and Beijing on **21 April 2026**, restoring air connectivity between the capitals of India and China. The move is expected to strengthen people-to-people ties, lower logistical costs in supply chains, and make travel easier for businesses in the technology and manufacturing sectors.

Earlier, on **2 October 2025**, the Government of India approved the revival of direct air services between the two countries after a five-year interruption. Flights had been halted in **March 2020** due to the global COVID-19 outbreak and the suspension of international travel. While services with several other countries resumed over time, flights to China stayed inactive amid tensions along the **Line of Actual Control (LAC)**. In **July 2025**, India also resumed issuing tourist visas to Chinese citizens.

## First India–China Bilateral Discussions on SCO Affairs

The first bilateral consultations between India and China on matters relating to the **Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO)** were held in **New Delhi on 16–17 April 2026**.

Representatives from both countries reviewed the implementation of decisions taken by SCO leaders and discussed the organisation's future direction. The two sides reaffirmed their commitment to maintaining dialogue and enhancing cooperation on issues connected with the SCO.
