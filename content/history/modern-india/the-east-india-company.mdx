# The East India Company

The British East India Company was founded in 1600 as a trading company seeking spices and textiles from the East. Over the next two centuries, it transformed from a merchant enterprise into the most powerful political force in India.

## From Trade to Power

The Company's transformation from trader to ruler began with the **Battle of Plassey in 1757**, where Robert Clive defeated the Nawab of Bengal. This victory gave the Company control over the wealth of Bengal and set it on the path to territorial conquest.

## Expansion Across India

Through a combination of military force, alliances, and policies such as the Doctrine of Lapse, the Company steadily expanded its control across the subcontinent until it governed most of India.

## The End of Company Rule

The Revolt of 1857 exposed the dangers of Company rule. In 1858, the British Crown took direct control of India, ending the Company's 250-year journey from trade to empire.
