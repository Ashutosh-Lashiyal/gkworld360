# The Government of India Act 1858

Passed in the aftermath of the Revolt of 1857, the Government of India Act 1858 was a landmark law that ended the rule of the British East India Company and transferred control of India directly to the British Crown.

## Key Provisions

The Act abolished the East India Company and placed India under the direct governance of the British government. A new office, the **Secretary of State for India**, was created in Britain to oversee Indian affairs.

## Significance

This Act marked the formal beginning of the **British Raj** — direct Crown rule that would last until India's independence in 1947. It reshaped the entire administrative structure of British India.
